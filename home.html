Life Below Water Read More

WHY WE CHOSE THIS GOAL

The ocean covers 70% of the surface of the earth. In that vastly unexplored world, we have fish as our food source, ocean habitats that protect us from storms, and planktons that produce our oxygen. Together, we have to protect them to sustain our livelihood and protect the marine life.

What we can do:

Fish Responsibly: Fishing is a major cause of our oceans decline in population. Although it does not mean fishing is bad, it becomes a problem once we fish more than we need or what we call, overfishing, because it massively decreases the ocean's fish population according to the WWF.

Reduce Waste: Currently, there are about 50-75 trillion pieces of plastic and microplastics in the ocean. Most of which are ingestible plastics by marine life mistaking it for food. Reducing waste is the easiest way we could start cleaning our oceans. Cutting down on single use plastic can help reduce marine pollution.

Conserve Habitats: This goal correlates with the others, because our ocean habitats are affected by the things that we do upshore. Restoring previously damaged ecosystems helps replenish the oceans population, leading to a healthy environment.
